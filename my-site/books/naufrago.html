Relato de un náufrago

by Gabriel García Márquez

Began reading: 14-12-2025

Status: reading

My thoughts while reading

Chapter 1. An admirable hability of García Márquez is how he can begin a book by 'spoiling' the ending —it literally being described in the title— and still make it a captivating story. Like in his Crónica de una muerte anunciada, he manages to be constantly refering to the tragic destiny of its characters in a way that still makes the reader wonder what will happen next. In particular, I admire how he can weave in premonitions and signs of what's to come without it sounding trivial or silly. He referts to death in a simple yet grave manner, which only increases the horror as pages go by while knowing the ending.

El día que yo me maree, ese día se marea el mar tr.: the day I get seasick, that day the sea gets sick

This quote is such a perfect example of this: it plays on what we know will happen without vanalizing it, the premonition serving solely as a dagger, sinking every time we're reminded where the story is headed.

16.12.25

Chapter 3. The accident has a clean retelling. Simple. There's no storm, there's only mild confussion, not enough to sell onto the idea that something bad is about to happen. Only in a matter of lines —in a matter of minutes— four men die at sea. They simply sink, disappear, to never be seen again. It's as chilling as it's plain.

18.12.25

Chapter 10. A silly observation but: we know by the title that our protagonist won't be saved until the 10th day. Yet, the way the story progresses makes us believe at times that, maybe, he will be saved before that. That is, until the 9th day, where we are led to almost lose hope.

20.12.25

Chapter 10. I have read and experienced many thoughts of death. To desire death is an incredibly tragic thing, though the one desiring it may not think so at the time.

The book's description if this death wish is like not our ordinary one: it's not carried over by clinical depression but by rationality, almost. The knowledge that it's most unlikely and improbable that one will be saved.
